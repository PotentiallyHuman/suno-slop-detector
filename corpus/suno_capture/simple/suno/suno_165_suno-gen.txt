[Verse 1]
I stamp your name in wax
On paper gone soft with brine
Fourteen nights in the lantern room
And still you answer on time
I watch the trawlers roll
Black backs through the harbor mouth
Your hands in the froth, your mouth in the wind
Your kiss in the driftwood south
[Pre-Chorus]
I write by the cracked red lamp
While the rain taps out your tune
If you come in from the deep tonight
I’ll leave the door unlatched for you
[Chorus]
Sea, don’t keep him long
Sea, bring him home
Sea, I’ve loved you wrong
Now let me go
Sea, take my ring
Take the ache I sowed
Sea, if you love me
Send him through the road
[Verse 2]

And tar on my cuffed-up sleeve
I press your letters to my chest
Like a child with a fistful of eaves
The lamps burn low at dawn
The glass gets cloudy and warm
I know your footsteps by the swell
By the way the whole house leans in storms
