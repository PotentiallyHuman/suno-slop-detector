[Verse 1]
We hauled a crate at dawn
From a dock that smelled of tar
Sealed in wire and wax
Stamped with a broken star
Old Mate said, “Don't split it open”
Then the hull began to groan
And the fruit rolled red as embers
In the dark below the foam
[Pre-Chorus]
One for the rope, boys
Two for the rain
Three for the little yellow devils
Stowed in chains
They sang in the hold
Like a deckhand lost at sea
And every bitten orange
Left salt on my teeth
[Chorus]
Ho, cursed oranges!
Roll, cursed oranges!
We ate one slice
And the storm said, “More!”
Ho, cursed oranges!
Bold cursed oranges!
The whole damn hold
Went wild below the floor
[Verse 2]
The cook cut one clean open
And the rind began to steam
Then the lanterns swung like pendulums
And the bilge turned green
The bosun bit the second
Spat blood into the brine
Said, “I know that sweet smell, mates
That’s trouble dressed as wine”
[Pre-Chorus]
One for the rope, boys
Two for the mast
Three for the yellow warning
That was nailed to the crate at last
They sang in the hold
When the moon climbed high and thin
And every cursed little segment
Breathed the wind back in
[Chorus]
Ho, cursed oranges!
Roll, cursed oranges!
We ate one slice
And the storm said, “More!”
Ho, cursed oranges!
Bold cursed oranges!
The whole damn hold
Went wild below the floor
[Bridge]
Then the captain laughed too loud
With juice on his chin
He tossed one overboard
And the sea bit in
Now the waves wear peels
And the gulls won’t leave us be
If you hear sweet fruit in the dark
Keep your hands from the sea
[Final Chorus]
Ho, cursed oranges!
Roll, cursed oranges!
We ate one slice
And the storm said, “More!”
Ho, cursed oranges!
Bold cursed oranges!
We’ll haul ’em home
Then sink them in the shore!
