[Verse 1]
At the end of Miller Lane
there’s one green crown left
between the cracked-up curbs
and the shuttered hardware set
Its roots hold tighter
than the old brick ground
while the rest of town
keeps sinking down
[Pre-Chorus]
Kids name it lucky
old men call it fate
I pass it every evening
through the chain-link gate
One small shade
on a hard, hard block
One small breath
where the pavement stops
[Chorus]
Last tree standing
hold on, hold on
Last tree standing
stay for us long
When the wind comes round
and the seasons lean
you keep one piece
of what we used to be
[Verse 2]
The barber shop is a dented shell
the church bell rusted still
paint flakes off the bakery sign
like snow on a windowsill
But your leaves keep talking
in the heat and dust
like something in this town
still remembers us
[Pre-Chorus]

in the bent tin tags

on a hard, hard block
One small breath
where the pavement stops

Last tree standing
hold on, hold on
Last tree standing

[Bridge]
I heard the planner said
this place was done
all straight roads, no roots
and a cheaper sun
But I knelt in the dirt
by your split-bark side
and I swear I felt
the town still alive
[Final Chorus]
Last tree standing
hold on, hold on
Last tree standing
