[Verse 1]
I seen the dawn spill copper
On the back of the waking sea
It looked like a coin in the gutter
Rolled loose by the moon for me
Your hand on the rail, old friend
You said, “What does morning know?”
I said, “It burns like a match struck low
Then softens into gold”
[Pre-Chorus]
Blue comes first
Like a held breath
Then the green starts moving in
Deep as a prayer
Under your skin
Where the cold old waters begin
[Chorus]
Blue on blue, my friend
Blue on blue again
(Blue on blue)
Blue like a whale-road wound
Blue like a storm cloud breaking through
[Verse 2]
At noon the sea turns glasswork
With a sharp white knife of sun
And the waves wear long bright collars
Like every edge got spun
But by the lee side, listen
There’s a bottle-green shade below
The kind that hides a whole lost harbor
And all the names we know
[Pre-Chorus]
Red runs rare
When the sky grows mean
A bruise at the edge of day
Then black rolls out
Like velvet smoke
And every gull flies away
[Chorus]
Blue on blue, my friend
Blue on blue again
(Blue on blue)
Blue like a whale-road wound
Blue like a storm cloud breaking through
[Bridge]
I know you cannot see it
But I swear it’s there for you
The tin-grey skin of rainwater
The jade in the deeper blue
And if the night comes heavy
I’ll name it till it glows
For every dark on the horizon
Has a color in my throat
[Final Chorus]
Blue on blue, my friend
Blue on blue again
(Blue on blue)
Blue like a whale-road wound
Blue like a storm cloud breaking through
Blue on blue, my friend
Blue on blue till the end
(Blue on blue)
I’ll paint the sea in words
Till you can hold it in your hands
